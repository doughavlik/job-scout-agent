## Explanation of Master Resume

This is my master resume.

A master resume, often referred to as a "kitchen sink" resume, is a comprehensive, living document that catalogs your entire professional history. Unlike a standard one or two-page resume tailored for a specific job, a master resume includes every position held, detailed descriptions of all responsibilities, every project completed, all certifications, and a full list of skills. 

The primary purpose of a master resume is to serve as a central repository of information. When applying for a specific role, you can quickly copy and paste the most relevant sections into a new, targeted document, ensuring that no significant achievements are forgotten while saving time on rewriting core descriptions.

Note that many parts of this resume has items that are largely duplicates of other items with slight variation of context or word choice.
