# Job Search Preferences

## Target Roles (in order of priority)

1. Customer Success Manager
2. Customer Experience Manager
3. Head of Customer Success
4. People Manager in Customer Success (e.g., Director/VP of CS, CS Team Lead)
5. Customer Success Operations Manager
6. Revenue Operations Manager
7. Sales Operations Manager
8. Technical Customer Success Manager
9. Implementation Manager
10. Forward Deployed Engineer

### Role Matching Notes

- Close variations of the above titles are acceptable (e.g., "Client Success Manager," "Customer Operations Lead," "RevOps Analyst" at a senior level).
- For **Forward Deployed Engineer**: evaluate fit based on generative AI skills, API integration experience, and the candidate's software engineering background (BS in Computer Science from Rice University, 4 years as a software engineer, 2 years as a Technical Account Manager). Weight practical AI/vibe-coding skills heavily for this role.

## Location Requirements

### Home Base
- Houston, Texas (current residence)

### Willing to Relocate To
- Austin, Texas
- Nashville, Tennessee

### Work Arrangement Rules
| Arrangement | Acceptable? | Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Remote | Yes | Must allow residence in Houston, Austin, or Nashville |
| Hybrid | Yes | Office must be within **75 miles** of Houston, Austin, or Nashville |
| On-site / In-office | Yes | Office must be within **75 miles** of Houston, Austin, or Nashville |

- If a posting says "remote" with no state/city restrictions, treat it as acceptable.
- If a posting restricts remote work to specific states, it must include **Texas** or **Tennessee**.

## Compensation

- **Minimum salary threshold:** Apply if the posted maximum salary is **at least $100,000/year**.
- **Salary not listed:** Still apply — do not disqualify jobs missing salary info.

## Seniority / Experience Level

- **Too junior:** Skip roles labeled "entry level," "associate," or requiring fewer than 3 years of experience.
- **Too senior:** Nothing is too senior — apply if the resume demonstrates strong qualification.
- Let the resume speak for itself when judging seniority fit.

## Industry

- **Open to any industry**, with one important caveat:
- **Skip roles that require deep domain experience the candidate lacks.** For example, if a job explicitly requires "5+ years in healthcare" and the resume shows no substantive healthcare experience, it is not a strong fit.
- Use the resume to judge industry fit rather than a fixed allow/deny list.

## Company Size

- No preference. Startups, mid-market, and enterprise/Fortune 500 are all acceptable.

## Deal-Breakers

- None specified. Evaluate every job on its merits against the resume.

## Scoring Guidance for the AI Agent

When scoring a job against these preferences:

1. **Title match** — Is the job title (or a close variant) in the target roles list?
2. **Location/arrangement match** — Does it satisfy the location and work arrangement rules above?
3. **Salary match** — Does it meet the $100K minimum (or have no salary listed)?
4. **Seniority match** — Is it at the right level (not entry-level/junior)?
5. **Resume fit** — Based on the candidate's actual experience, is this a realistic and strong application? Pay special attention to required years of domain-specific experience.
6. **Industry fit** — Does the posting require niche domain experience not reflected in the resume?

Weight factors 1, 2, and 5 most heavily.
